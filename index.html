AI LinkedIn Lead Engagement Engine

A multi-workflow system that scales founder-led LinkedIn engagement across hundreds of prospects — acting human, staying safe, and turning passive engagement into pipeline.

n8nApifyUnipileAWS BedrockGPT-4oGoogle Sheets

Business problem

FortyGuard needed to scale founder-led outreach to hundreds of targeted prospects without burning out the team — or getting the account flagged by LinkedIn. The system had to act human, sound human, and stay within platform safety limits.

Technical challenges

  • Generating comments that read as written, not generated — a persona prompt producing 1–3 line, human-tone replies with no generic praise.
  • Staying under platform radar with randomized behavioral pacing and controlled execution windows.
  • Preventing duplicate comments and double-fires across 5 daily runs via status tracking + dedup.
  • Managing session/cookie auth and API rate limits reliably at scale.

Lessons learned

Generation was the easy 20%. The 80% that made it production-worthy was reliability — pacing, deduplication, and safe re-runs. AI is a component; the system around it is the job.

WhatsApp → CRM Business-Card Pipeline

Snap a business card, send it to a WhatsApp number, and it becomes a clean, deduplicated CRM contact in seconds — no manual re-typing, ever.

WhatsApp Business APIGoogle Vision OCRLLMHubSpotGoogle Drive

Business problem

The team collected business cards at events and meetings, then lost them or manually retyped every field into HubSpot. The pipeline had to be frictionless and reliable enough to trust with lead data.

Technical challenges

  • OCR output is noisy — an LLM after OCR normalizes raw text into strict, CRM-safe JSON.
  • Deduplication on email/phone so re-sent cards update rather than duplicate contacts.
  • Explicit handling of every failure mode: missing image, OCR failure, malformed LLM output, CRM API error.

Lessons learned

Chaining an LLM behind OCR is what turned an unreliable text dump into structured data safe to write to a CRM. Validation gates before every external write prevent silent corruption.

AI Contact Segmentation at 25k Scale

Turned 25,000+ email-only HubSpot contacts into a targetable, segmented database using domain grouping and website-content LLM classification — with a cost gate baked in.

n8nAzure OpenAIHubSpotWeb scrapingLLM classification

Business problem

HubSpot held 25,000+ contacts with nothing but an email address — no industry or segment — so targeted campaigns were impossible and manual sorting didn't scale.

Technical challenges

  • Cost control at scale: grouping by email domain avoided redundant company lookups; a webhook variant gated AI to business domains only, skipping free/personal domains.
  • Token-budgeted website truncation to keep classification cheap and fast.
  • Fallback logic for inconsistent LLM output so every contact always resolves to a category.

Lessons learned

Cost is a design decision, not an afterthought. Domain grouping + a business-domain gate cut LLM calls dramatically while keeping quality — the difference between a viable pipeline and an expensive one.

Data Engineering · Batching · Cost Efficiency

LinkedIn Enrichment at Scale — 4,740 → 10 calls

A batched enrichment pipeline that processed 4,740 profiles in ~10 API executions instead of 4,740 — a textbook cost/throughput win, scalable to 100k+.

n8nApifyGoogle SheetsSplit in Batches

Business problem

4,740 LinkedIn profiles needed location/company enrichment. A naïve one-call-per-profile approach meant 4,740 slow, expensive scraper runs.

Technical challenges

  • Aggregating profiles into batches of 500 per Apify actor call via Split-in-Batches.
  • Per-record edge cases — not found / private / missing URL — resolved with explicit statuses.
  • Batch-level retries so one failure doesn't sink the run.

Lessons learned

Batching is the cheapest performance win in automation. Always ask "can this be one call instead of N?" — here it turned 4,740 calls into ~10.

[05] / How I Build

Reliability is the feature.

The same engineering discipline goes into every workflow — because the ones that matter run unattended.

01

Map the failure modes

Before building, I list what breaks: rate limits, malformed LLM output, duplicate triggers, missing data, silent failures — then design for each.

02

Make it idempotent

Append-or-update by unique key, Math.max merges, preserved terminal states — so any workflow is safe to re-run at the worst moment.

03

Constrain the AI

Structured output parsers, strict JSON schemas, fallbacks, and token budgets keep LLM steps reliable and cheap — not a coin flip.

04

Keep humans in the loop

Drafts instead of auto-send, WhatsApp alerts on stale deals, approval queues — a person stays in the loop exactly where judgment matters.
